About the Municipality

Welcome to Imelda

Imelda, officially the Municipality of Imelda (Cebuano: Lungsod sa Imelda; Chavacano: Municipalidad de Imelda; Tagalog: Bayan ng Imelda), is a municipality in the province of Zamboanga Sibugay, Philippines. According to the 2024 census, it has a population of 29,652 people. The municipality is the least populous in the province and the third smallest in area.

Explore & Discover

Tourist Attractions

Imelda, Zamboanga Sibugay, is an emerging eco-cultural destination marketed as the "Gateway to Culture and Beauty." Though one of the least populous municipalities in the province, it is recognized for its peaceful, community-driven atmosphere and its role as a strategic jumping-off point for exploring Zamboanga Sibugay's broader natural wonders.

Eco-Cultural & Agri-Tourism
Viente Uno Park and Mountain Resort

A popular hilltop farm tourism site in Sta. Barbara recognized for its spectacular sunrise viewing. It serves as a hub for family-oriented outdoor activities, including mountain biking, hiking, and relaxing in a "chill" atmosphere with local coffee.

Adventure & Physical Activity
Baluran Waterfalls

A serene natural attraction located in the neighboring Barangay Baluran. It features refreshing waters and is a popular spot for local families and nature enthusiasts seeking a quick dip or a picnic.

Scenic & Photography
Baluyan Peak

A scenic highland viewpoint famous for its breathtaking 360-degree sunset vistas and frequent morning "sea of clouds." It offers a moderate trekking experience through ancestral Subanen lands, making it a favorite spot for back-to-basics camping and nature photography.

Heritage & Tradition

Culture & Traditions

Imelda's cultural life is a vibrant tapestry woven from rural Filipino traditions, faith-rooted practices, and a growing spirit of civic pride — expressed most vividly through its festivals, homegrown food, and the warm hospitality of its people.

  • 🎉
    Imeldahan, Bahandianon & Lechon Festivals
    Imelda celebrates key festivals blending local identity, food culture, and community spirit. The Bahandianon and Lechon Festivals were held in 2024, highlighting agricultural roots and love for festive food with street dancing and traditional activities. The Imeldahan Festival unites barangays in town pride annually.
  • 🍽️
    Home‑style Filipino Food
    Imelda's food culture centers on hearty Filipino comfort fare — rice meals with grilled fish and chicken from roadside eateries, bakeshops, balut stalls, and a cherished herbal turmeric-based wellness drink made from wild turmeric and sugar.
  • 🎭
    Homegrown Creations
    Local art is practical and spirited — rattan and wood furniture, native hats, baskets, and decorative mirrors sold in roadside shops. Music fills fiestas and barangay gatherings with cover songs, folk tunes, and drum-and-lyre school performances.
  • 🏛️
    Faith‑Rooted Traditions
    Cultural practices are anchored in Catholic and rural Filipino traditions — respect for elders, Holy Week observances, patron-saint fiestas, and deep-seated values around hospitality, hard work, and neighbor-helping-neighbor.

Livelihood & Industry

Local Economy

Economic activities in Imelda are primarily anchored on agriculture, small-scale food production, and local trade. The town's economy relies heavily on farming — rice, corn, fruits, and vegetables — along with fishing, cottage industries, and roadside commerce that keep the community self-sufficient and closely tied to everyday trade and homegrown enterprises.

🌾
Agriculture

The primary industry — most families depend on rice, corn, fruits, and vegetables grown on lowland and upland farms supplying both local consumption and nearby markets.

🐟
Fishing & Aquaculture

Coastal and riverside barangays harvest fish and freshwater products that support household food security and small-scale trade throughout the town.

🌿
Food & Craft Manufacturing

Balut production, bakeshops, rattan and wood furniture, native hats, baskets, and decorative items sold in shops and along the national highway.

🏪
Trade & Retail Services

Sari-sari stores, roadside stalls, transport services, and eateries support daily commerce and link agricultural, fishing, and craft sectors to local customers.

Local Government Unit

Municipal Officials

The LGU of Imelda is dedicated to fostering a vibrant, self-reliant, and agro-industrialized community under transparent and participatory governance. Key programs include Project LAWA at BINHI for agricultural resilience, solar-powered infrastructure, expansion of Imelda District Hospital, digitized eBPLS services, and Level II Water Systems for every barangay.
